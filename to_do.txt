

### **1. Equity-Adjusted Learning Gap (EALG)**  
**What it measures**: How much of the variance in **Puntaje Global** is *unexplained* by socioeconomic status (SES quintile, parental education, household income proxy).  
**Formula**:  
\[
EALG = 1 - R^2_{\text{SES model}}
\]  
where SES model regresses Global Score on SES quintile + parental education + urban/rural.  
**Target**: >0.85 (i.e., <15% of score variance due to SES).  
**Why independent**: Isolates school/system value-added *after* controlling for family background. High correlation with raw averages would invalidate independence.

### **3. Ethnic Resilience Ratio (ERR)**  
**What it measures**: Performance of **indigenous and afro-Colombian students** relative to national average **in Ciencias Naturales**, *conditional on SES and rurality*.  
**Formula**:  
\[
ERR = \frac{\bar{X}_{\text{indigenous/afro, matched}}}{\bar{X}_{\text{national average}}}
\]  
(using propensity score matching on SES, rural/urban, school type).  
**Target**: >0.95.  
**Why independent**: Science scores less influenced by language bias (vs. Lectura CrÃ­tica) and reveal ethno-education policy impact.

### **5. Municipal Efficiency Frontier (MEF)**  
**What it measures**: Percentage of municipalities in the **top decile of Puntaje Global per peso of per-student public spending** (using Saber 11 + Ministry budget data linkage by school).  
**Formula**:  
\[
MEF = \% \text{ municipalities with } \frac{\text{Global Score}}{\text{Spending per student}} > P90_{\text{national}}
\]  
**Target**: >15%.  
**Why independent**: Efficiency (output/input) is conceptually distinct from equity, rural gaps, or subject-specific performance.

---

### **6. School-Level Volatility Stabilizer (SVS)**  
**What it measures**: Year-to-year instability in **school-level Sociales y Ciudadanas** rankings (percentile), signaling curriculum or teacher turnover.  
**Formula**:  
\[
SVS = 1 - \text{median} |\text{Rank}_{t} - \text{Rank}_{t-1}| \text{ across official schools}
\]  
**Target**: >0.80 (low volatility).  
**Why independent**: Civics education is uniquely sensitive to principal/teacher stability and local politics; low correlation with global scores or demographics.
